IgA nephropathy is the most common primary glomerulonephritis worldwide, also relatively common in children. It is characterized by the deposition of immunoglobulin A (IgA) in the glomerular mesangium.

## What is IgA Nephropathy?

IgA nephropathy is an autoimmune-related kidney disease. When abnormal IgA antibodies deposit in the kidney's filtering units (glomeruli), they cause inflammation and kidney damage.

## Common Symptoms

- Gross hematuria: tea-colored or red urine, often appearing after upper respiratory infections
- Microscopic hematuria: not visible to the eye but detected in urine tests
- Proteinuria: protein in the urine
- Some children may develop high blood pressure

## Diagnosis

Definitive diagnosis requires a kidney biopsy, where immunofluorescence examination reveals IgA deposits in the glomerular mesangium.

## Treatment and Management

Mild cases are managed with observation and regular monitoring. For children with significant proteinuria or impaired kidney function, ACEI/ARB medications, corticosteroids, or immunosuppressants may be needed.

## Lifestyle Tips

- Prevent upper respiratory infections
- Exercise moderately, avoid overexertion
- Follow a low-salt diet
- Regular follow-up to monitor kidney function
