For children with kidney disease, proper daily care is an important part of treatment. Good lifestyle management can reduce relapses and promote recovery.

## Diet Management

### Salt Control
Children with kidney disease should limit salt intake to 2-3 grams per day. Avoid pickled foods, instant noodles, and other high-salt items.

### Protein Intake
During edema and heavy proteinuria, protein intake should be moderately controlled, focusing on high-quality proteins (eggs, fish, lean meat). Normal diet can resume during remission.

### Adequate Calories
Ensure sufficient carbohydrates and moderate fat intake to meet children's growth and development needs.

## Exercise and Rest

- Bed rest during acute phases
- Moderate exercise during remission (walking, light swimming)
- Avoid strenuous exercise and overexertion
- Ensure adequate sleep

## Infection Prevention

Infection is a major trigger for relapse:
- Wash hands frequently
- Avoid crowded places
- Keep up with vaccinations (consult your doctor)
- Isolate from family members with colds

## Emotional Support

Long-term illness can affect children's mental health. Parents should:
- Provide ample love and encouragement
- Help children understand their condition
- Maintain normal school and social activities
- Seek counseling if needed

## Regular Follow-up

- Follow doctor's schedule for urine and kidney function tests
- Record daily urine output and weight changes
- Take medications as prescribed
- Seek immediate care if swelling worsens or urine output decreases
